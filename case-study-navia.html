The challenge

Navia Logistics was growing but experiencing the pain points that come with growth in freight forwarding: workloads that spiked unpredictably, processes that were hard to hand off, and a team that was skilled but not operating from a shared system.

The goal was to reduce operational pain, make work more visible, and give the team a shared framework for thinking through problems — not just a new dashboard.

The problem.

Growth creates complexity. As Navia's volume increased, the informal coordination mechanisms that worked at smaller scale stopped working. Work was being done in the wrong order. Some team members were overloaded while others had capacity. Problems were being discovered at the worst possible moment — when the customer was already affected.

The underlying issue wasn't the team. It was the absence of a system that made the flow visible and gave people a shared way of deciding what to work on next.

The work.

ViAGO implemented CargoWise productivity tools across the Navia operation — configuring the workflow engine to make work visible, prioritised, and balanced across the team.

Alongside the CargoWise implementation, ViAGO delivered Black Belt in Thinking (BBIT) training across the business. The aim: give the team the thinking skills to use the new system well, and to handle the judgement calls that no system can make for them.

The combination matters. A productivity tool tells you what's happening. BBIT gives your people the thinking skills to decide what to do about it.

What changed.

"Thus far results have been very positive and we have seen a lot of our initial pain points disappear or substantially reduced."

Navia Logistics

  • Initial pain points substantially reduced across the operation.
  • Work became visible and prioritised — team members knew what to work on next.
  • BBIT gave the team a shared framework for problem-solving that continued to pay dividends beyond the implementation.
